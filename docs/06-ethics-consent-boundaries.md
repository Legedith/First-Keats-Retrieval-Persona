# Ethics, Consent, and Boundaries

## Core rule

The narrator’s control is not a courtesy. It is the foundation of the method.

Do not record first and ask permission later.

## Consent is ongoing

Consent should exist at several levels:

1. Consent to have the conversation.
2. Consent to record.
3. Consent to transcribe.
4. Consent to translate.
5. Consent to summarize.
6. Consent to store.
7. Consent to share with specific people.
8. Consent to publish or donate.
9. Consent to use after death.
10. Consent to use in any future AI or digital system.

These are not the same thing.

A person may agree to be recorded but not shared. They may agree to family sharing but not public release. They may agree to transcription but not voice cloning. They may agree today and change their mind later.

## Pre-interview agreement

Before recording, cover:

> “What are we trying to preserve?”  
> “Who may listen?”  
> “Who may not listen?”  
> “Are there topics we should avoid?”  
> “Are there topics where I should ask before continuing?”  
> “Can you pause or stop at any time?”  
> “Can you later restrict, seal, or remove material?”  
> “What should happen to this material after death or incapacity?”

Use [`templates/pre_interview_agreement.md`](../templates/pre_interview_agreement.md).

## Topic boundaries

Classify topics into four groups.

### Open

The narrator is comfortable discussing.

### Ask first

The interviewer must pause and ask permission before entering.

Example:

> “Would it be okay to ask about your marriage, or should we leave that for another time?”

### Closed

Do not ask.

### Unknown

Treat as sensitive until clarified.

## Off-record rule

If the narrator says “off the record,” recording should stop.

Do not keep a hidden transcript. Do not write detailed notes unless the narrator agrees. Do not later summarize the off-record material.

A true off-record boundary changes the record itself.

## Sharing levels

Each recording, clip, transcript, or story can have its own sharing level.

Suggested levels:

- Narrator only.
- Interviewer only.
- Named people only.
- Immediate family.
- Extended family.
- All descendants.
- Public.
- Sealed until a date.
- Sealed until after death.
- Permanently private.

Sensitive stories should default to narrower access.

## Third-party privacy

A narrator can tell a story involving another person, but that does not erase the other person’s privacy.

Be careful with:

- Medical history.
- Mental health.
- Addiction.
- Violence.
- Abuse.
- Sexual relationships.
- Paternity or adoption.
- Money, debt, inheritance.
- Crime or accusations.
- Political risk.
- Caste, religion, migration, or identity-based risk.
- Stories about living people who cannot respond.

Consider marking such material restricted by default.

## Family power dynamics

Family interviews are not neutral.

A narrator may self-censor because the interviewer is:

- A child.
- A parent.
- A spouse.
- A sibling.
- A person from a more powerful gender, caste, class, religion, or generation.
- Someone who benefits from a particular version of the story.
- Someone who was part of the painful event.

The method should allow multiple interviewers and private additions.

## Difficult material

Do not chase trauma.

If the narrator becomes distressed:

> “Would you like to continue, pause, change topics, or stop?”

Do not say:

> “This will help you heal.”

Do not say:

> “You need closure.”

Do not say:

> “People should know the truth.”

The narrator controls the boundary.

## After difficult material

Before ending, ask:

> “Do you want any restrictions on what we just discussed?”  
> “Do you want this kept, sealed, or removed?”  
> “Should we return to something lighter before stopping?”

End with stabilization, not intensity.

## For narrators with cognitive impairment

This method is not designed as a dementia, capacity, or clinical protocol.

If capacity is uncertain:

- Use shorter sessions.
- Use simpler consent checks.
- Involve legally appropriate decision-makers.
- Avoid sensitive topics unless the person clearly wants to speak.
- Preserve dignity.
- Do not pressure exact recall.
- Seek professional guidance where needed.

## For minors

This method is designed for adults.

Do not use it with minors without a separate child-safe protocol, guardian consent where legally required, and safeguards against coercion.

## Future AI use

Do not imply future AI use is covered by general interview consent.

Ask separately.

Future AI use may include:

- Transcription.
- Translation.
- Summarization.
- Search.
- Voice synthesis.
- Persona simulation.
- Training models.

Each requires explicit permission.

Default rule:

> No synthetic voice, avatar, or first-person persona unless the narrator explicitly consented while alive and capable.
