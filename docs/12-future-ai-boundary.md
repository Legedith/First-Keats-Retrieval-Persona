# Future AI Boundary

This repository is intentionally human-first.

Transcription, translation, summarization, search, and interactive retrieval may become useful later. They should not redefine the first phase.

## Current phase

The current phase is:

- Human interviewer.
- Human narrator.
- Human consent.
- Human judgment.
- Non-technical method.
- Manual coverage tracking.
- Manual question selection.
- Manual ethics and boundaries.

## Acceptable future AI roles

Future AI may help with:

- Transcription.
- Translation.
- Indexing.
- Finding names, places, and repeated themes.
- Suggesting non-leading follow-up questions.
- Checking interviewer questions for unsafe wording.
- Creating draft summaries for human review.
- Helping listeners search the archive.
- Flagging uncertain claims.
- Preparing follow-up coverage maps.

## Unacceptable default AI roles

AI should not default to:

- Pretending to be the narrator.
- Speaking as the narrator in first person.
- Creating statements the narrator never made.
- Resolving contradictions without human review.
- Inferring private emotions from voice, face, or style.
- Pressuring disclosure.
- Deciding sharing permissions.
- Reusing recordings for model development without explicit consent.
- Making therapeutic claims.

## The line between archive and persona

An archive answers:

> “What did she say about leaving home?”

A persona-like interface answers:

> “I left home because…”

The second is far more risky. It can become plausible fiction.

Default future interface should be:

> “The archive contains three relevant clips. In one, she says…”

not:

> “I remember leaving home…”

## AI feedback rules for interviewers

If AI is later used to coach interviewers, it should follow these rules:

1. Flag leading questions.
2. Flag multi-part questions.
3. Flag introduced emotion.
4. Flag introduced motive.
5. Flag premature fact-checking.
6. Flag pressure after refusal.
7. Suggest one safer rewrite.
8. Remind interviewer to ask about ordinary life.
9. Remind interviewer to ask about context.
10. Remind interviewer to pause after distress.
11. Never demand deeper disclosure.
12. Never optimize for emotional intensity.

## Consent for AI use

General recording consent is not AI consent.

Separate consent is needed for:

- Transcription.
- Translation.
- Summarization.
- Search indexing.
- Synthetic likeness or voice features.
- Persona-like interfaces.
- Model training or commercial reuse.

Default:

> No synthetic representation or persona-like interface without explicit opt-in.

## Source-bound generation rule

Any future AI summary should distinguish:

- Direct quote.
- Paraphrase.
- Summary.
- Inference.
- Uncertain claim.
- Disputed claim.
- Unsupported claim.

The system should be able to answer:

> “What clip supports this sentence?”

If it cannot, the sentence should not be presented as fact.

## Human review

All AI outputs that represent the narrator should be reviewed by:

1. The narrator, if possible.
2. A designated archive steward, if the narrator is unavailable.
3. A person aware of restrictions and context.

## The strongest future AI use

The safest and most useful future AI product is not a person-like bot.

It is an **Ask the Archive** interface.

Example:

Question:

> “What did my grandfather say about his first job?”

Answer:

> “There are two clips. In the first, he describes getting the job through his cousin. In the second, he describes his first salary. The exact year is uncertain.”

Then show the clips.

This preserves the difference between the person and the record.
