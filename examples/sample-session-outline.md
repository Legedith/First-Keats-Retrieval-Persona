# Sample Session Outline

This is a sample first session.

Adapt it to the narrator.

## Before recording

Say:

> “The purpose is to preserve your life story in your words. You can skip anything, pause, stop, or later restrict material. This is not a test of memory.”

Confirm:

- May I record?
- Who may hear this?
- Are any topics off-limits?
- Should I ask before entering sensitive topics?
- How long should we talk today?

## Opening

Ask:

> “If your life were divided into chapters, what would the chapters be called?”

Let the narrator answer freely.

Write chapter names exactly as given.

## Pick one chapter

Ask:

> “Which chapter would you like to open first?”

Do not automatically begin with childhood.

## Map lightly

Ask:

> “Where were you living then?”  
> “Who was part of your daily life?”  
> “What did most days revolve around?”

## Ask for ordinary life

Ask:

> “What did a normal day in that chapter look like?”

Follow up:

> “What happened in the morning?”  
> “Who did what?”  
> “What sounds, smells, or routines belonged to that time?”

## Ask for one scene

Ask:

> “Is there one particular incident from that period that stays with you?”

Follow up:

> “Where were you?”  
> “Who else was there?”  
> “What happened first?”  
> “What happened after that?”

## Ask for meaning

Only after the story is clear:

> “What did this mean to you then?”  
> “Do you understand it differently now?”

## Ask about context

Ask:

> “What choices did someone in your position have then?”  
> “What would people around you have expected?”

## Close

Ask:

> “What should I ask about next time?”  
> “Is there anything from today that should stay private?”  
> “Did I misunderstand anything?”  
> “What feels like a good place to stop?”
